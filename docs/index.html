Trading 101

Imagine money is points and trading is swapping

Read these in order. No chart words yet. Just the idea.

  1. 1

    Money is a way to trade

    Money is like points people agree to use. You can give points to get a toy, food, a game, or a tiny piece of a company.

  2. 2

    Price means how many points something costs

    If a snack costs 2 points today and 3 points tomorrow, the price went up. If it costs 1 point tomorrow, the price went down.

  3. 3

    Trading means trying to swap at a good time

    A trader tries to buy something before its price goes up, or sell something before its price goes down.

  4. 4

    Buy means "I think it may go up"

    You trade your money for the thing. If the price rises later, you may be able to sell it for more money.

  5. 5

    Sell means "I think it may go down"

    You get out of the thing, or in some markets you make a trade that wins if price falls. The simple idea is: sell points down.

  6. 6

    Wait means "do nothing yet"

    This is important. Good trading is not clicking all day. Many times the best move is to wait.

Now the chart

A chart is just a picture of price moving

Green candles mean up

Green usually means price moved higher during that little piece of time.

Red candles mean down

Red usually means price moved lower during that little piece of time.

An area is like a floor or ceiling

Sometimes price bounces near the same place. The indicator draws that place so it is easier to see.

The status label is the helper

It looks at the chart and gives a simple answer: Buy, Sell, Wait, or No Trade.

The simple checklist

The indicator waits before it speaks

  1. 1

    First it asks: which way is price walking?

    If price is walking up, it looks for a Buy. If price is walking down, it looks for a Sell.

  2. 2

    Then it asks: did price make a strong move?

    A strong move is like price taking a big step instead of a tiny step.

  3. 3

    Then it asks: did price come back?

    The marked area is the waiting spot. No touch means no trade yet.

  4. 4

    Last it asks: did price move the right way again?

    If yes, the label can say Buy or Sell. If no, it keeps saying Wait.

Use it on TradingView

Install the indicator

  1. Open TradingView and open Pine Editor.
  2. Copy the code from indicator/ChamilleiaSupplyDemand.pine.
  3. Paste it, save it, then choose Add to chart.
  4. Use the 5-minute chart if you want the setup from the written plan.
  5. Look at the Status box first: Buy, Sell, Wait, or No Trade.
